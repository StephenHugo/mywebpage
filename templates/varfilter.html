A variance filter returns the variance of pixel values within a certain neighborhood throughout the image. The easiest implementation is to calculate the average of squared pixel values and subtract those from the average pixel values squared... something like Average(I^2)-Average(I)^2, where I use a 5x5 Gaussian filter to average a neighborhood of pixels here. The result looks interesting, but is also typically used to enhance edges in images of cell cultures or histology, providing a means to segment cells from the background.
